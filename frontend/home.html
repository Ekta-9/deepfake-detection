Why This Matters

AI-generated media has become increasingly convincing and widely accessible. Deepfakes are being used to spread misinformation, impersonate individuals, and manipulate public perception — often with serious personal, political, and financial consequences. As synthetic media grows harder to distinguish from reality, tools that help verify authenticity are becoming essential.

What is a deepfake?

Deepfakes are AI-generated images or videos that convincingly replace a person's likeness with another's. Using GANs and diffusion models, they can be nearly indistinguishable from authentic media to the human eye.

How detection works

This tool uses a ResNet50-based classifier fine-tuned on real and AI-generated images. The GradCAM overlay highlights which regions influenced the prediction — typically eyes, skin texture, and hair boundaries where synthesis artifacts appear.
